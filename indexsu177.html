4.5.1 problem 69

Let Ij be the event that the j-th person in the sample is picked exactly once.

Let X=∑1000j=0Ij be the total number of people that are sampled once. Since P(Ij=1)=(106−1106)999, E(X)=∑1000j=0P(Ij=1)=∑1000j=0106−1106999≈999. Thus, the expected number of people sampled more than once is 1.

Let Z∼Poiss(1) approximate the number of people sampled more than once. Then, P(a person is sampled more than once)≈P(Z>0)=1−e−1≈0.63.
