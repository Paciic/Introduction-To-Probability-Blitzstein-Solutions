4.6.3 problem 86

(a)
P(X=x,Y=y,Z=z)=(nAx)(nBy)(nCz)(nm)
(b)
Let Ij be the indicator variable for person j in the sample being a member of party A. Then, X=∑mi=1Ii⇒E(X)=mnAn by symmetry.
(c)
Var(X)=E(X2)−(E(X))2=∑nAi=1(i2(nAi)(n−nAm−i)(nm))−(mnAn)2

When m=1, Var(X)=nAn−(nAn)2=nAn(1−nAn)=nAn×nB+nCn.

When m=n, then Var(X) should be 0, since all nA members of party A are sampled. However, the above expression for Var(X) doesn’t equal 0 when m=n, so it is probably incorrect.
