Let k be the median, since X is continuous from 1 onwards,
\[F(k) = 1/2\]
where,
\begin{align*}
F(k) &= \int_{-\infty}^{k}{\frac{a}{x^{a+1}}} \\
&= \int_{1}^{k}{\frac{a}{x^{a+1}}} \\
&= 1 - k^{-a}
\end{align*}
thus,
\begin{align*}
1-k^{-a} &= \frac{1}{2} \\
k &= \sqrt[\leftroot{-2}\uproot{2}a]{2}
\end{align*}
where F is the CDF of X. 

Let c be the mode, $f$ be the PDF of X, then,
\[f'(x) = -a(a+1)x^{-a-2}\]
since $f'(x) \le 0$ for all $x \in [1,\infty)$, $f'(x)$ is strictly decreasing, hence $f(x)$ is maximum at 1.
\[c=1\]
